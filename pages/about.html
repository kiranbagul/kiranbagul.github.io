The short version

A career in the hard corners.

I started out writing code — the kind that keeps you awake at 2am when a production box misbehaves. Eighteen years on, I still do. I've just learned that the harder problems usually aren't in the code.

Today I lead the Player Experience pillar at Entain (IVY Comptech) — a regulated multi-market consumer product serving millions. I authored the AI-First Delivery Playbook now used as the operating baseline across market squads, and I'm shaping AI Pods & Ways of Working as the next-generation squad model. I also helped scale the Entain Pune centre from 3 to 100 engineers over the years.

Before Entain, at Bank Jago (Dkatalis Labs), I was Engineering Team Lead and Pune Chapter Lead for a greenfield digital bank. I designed and built core banking ground-up — transfers, debit cards, wallet and bill payments, rewards — while scaling the Pune chapter from 5 engineers to 40, and defining the release engineering playbook later adopted org-wide.

Earlier years were at Zalando in Berlin (event-driven Kubernetes platforms for logistics automation at European e-commerce scale), Equal Experts (reactive Scala for India's largest broadband marketplace; resilient AWS IaC and CI-CD for a UK publisher), and earlier still Schlumberger, CDK Global (ADP), Cisco and UST Global — where I did CI-CD modernisation that took 10× off build times, distributed systems work, and spent time deep inside Cisco's networking stack.

I was a founding member of the William Hill India tech centre (which became Entain). I'm comfortable in code reviews and boardrooms alike; the only thing I insist on is that the engineering stays honest — to the architecture, to the customer, and to the people doing the work.

Away from work I'm an amateur astronomer, tinkerer and long-form reader. I built SkyWiki, an Android app for amateur astronomers that crossed 100K downloads and became a Google Play Editor's Choice. Curiosity is a professional skill.
